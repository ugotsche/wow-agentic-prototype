Woolworths — Concept Prototype

☀️ Start of Shift

The agent proactively briefs Sarah with her schedule, delivery info, training due, and team messages — pulled from UKG, SAP, SuccessFactors, and WorkJam automatically. She clocks in with a single tap.

🏖️ Leave Request

Sarah asks "Can I take next Friday off?" and the agent checks her leave balance (SuccessFactors), roster impact (UKG), and available cover — then submits the leave and messages a colleague to cover, all in one conversation.

⚠️ Safety Incident

Sarah reports a slip and the agent pre-fills the entire incident form from her profile, shift, and store location. No navigating to a separate SAP safety system.

📚 Learning

The agent knows Sarah has a training due and spots a quiet window in her roster to suggest doing it. Sets her WorkJam status to "In Training" automatically.

🔄 Shift Swap

Sarah says she can't work Wednesday. The agent finds 3 qualified, available colleagues who wouldn't hit overtime, sends the swap request, and handles the roster update.

The key thing this demonstrates is the orchestration layer concept — Sarah never opens UKG, SuccessFactors, SAP, or WorkJam directly. She just talks to the assistant in natural language, and it handles the cross-system coordination behind the scenes. Each scenario shows the source system tags so you can see how many systems are being stitched together invisibly.
